## 3. Terms and definitions

These are definitions from ISO 9000:2015 that support the ISO 13485 QMS.

**3.1 Terms related to person or people** 

| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Top management** | Person or group of people who directs and controls an organization at the highest level |
| **2** | **Quality management system consultant** | Person who assists the organization on QMS realization, giving advice or information |
| **3** | **Involvement** | Taking part in an activity, event or situation |
| **4** | **Engagement** | Involvement in, and contribution to, activities to achieve shared objectives |
| **5** | **Configuration authority** | Person or group of people with assigned responsibility and authority to make decisions on the configuration |
| **6** | **Dispute assistant** | Person assigned by a dispute-provider to assist the parties in resolving a dispute |



**3.2 Terms related to organization** 

| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Organization** | Person or group of people that has its own functions with responsibilities, authorities and relationships to achieve its objectives |
| **2** | **Context of the organization** | Combination of internal and external issues that can have an effect on an organization’s approach to developing and achieving its objectives |
| **3** | **Interested party** | Person or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by a decision or activity |
| **4** | **Customer** | Person or organization that could or does receive a product or a service that is intended for or required by this person or organization |
| **5** | **Provider** | Organization that provides a product or a service |
| **6** | **External provider** | Provider that is not part of the organization |
| **7** | **Dispute-provider** | Person or organization that supplies and operates an external dispute resolution process |
| **8** | **Association** | Organization consisting of member organizations or persons |


**3.3 Terms related to activity** 

| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Improvement** | Activity to enhance performance |
| **2** | **Continual improvement** | Recurring activity to enhance performance |
| **3** | **Management** | Coordinated activities to direct and control an organization |
| **4** | **Quality management** | Management with regard to quality |
| **5** | **Quality planning** | Part of quality management focused on setting quality objectives and specifying necessary operating processes and related resources to fulfil the quality objectives |
| **6** | **Quality assurance** | Part of quality management focused on providing confidence that quality requirements will be fulfilled |
| **7** | **Quality control** | Part of quality management focused on fulfilling quality requirements |
| **8** | **Quality improvement** | Part of quality management focused on increasing the ability to fulfil quality requirements |
| **9** | **Configuration management** | Coordinated activities to direct and control configuration |
| **10** | **Change control** | Activities for control of the output after formal approval of its product configuration information |
| **11** | **Activity** | Smallest identified object of work in a project |
| **12** | **Project management** | Planning, organizing, monitoring, controlling and reporting of all aspects of a project, and the motivation of all those involved in it to achieve the project objectives |
| **13** | **Configuration object** | Object within a configuration that satisfies an end-use function |



**3.4 Terms related to process** 
| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Process** | Interrelated or interacting activities which transforms inputs into outputs |
| **2** | **Project** | Unique process, consisting of a set of coordinated and controlled activities with start and finish dates, undertaken to achieve an objective conforming to specific requirements |
| **3** | **QMS realization** | Process of establishing, documenting, implementing, maintaining and continually improving a quality management system |
| **4** | **Aompetence acquisition** | Process of attaining competence |
| **5** | **Procedure** | Specified way to carry out an activity or a process |
| **6** | **Outsource (verb)** | Make an arrangement where an external organization performs part of an organization’s function or process |
| **7** | **Quality manual** | Specification for the quality management system of an organization |
| **8** | **Strategic level** | Level at which top management activities are performed |



**3.5 Terms related to system** 

| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **System** | Set of interrelated or interacting elements |
| **2** | **Infrastructure** | System of facilities, equipment and services needed for the operation of an organization |
| **3** | **Management system** | Set of interrelated or interacting elements of an organization to establish policies and objectives, and processes to achieve those objectives |
| **4** | **Quality management system** | Part of a management system with regard to quality |
| **5** | **Work environment** | Set of conditions under which work is performed |
| **6** | **Metrological confirmation** | Set of operations required to ensure that measuring equipment conforms to the requirements for its intended use |
| **7** | **Measurement system** | Set of measuring instruments and other devices, or substances, assembled and, where appropriate, connected to form a measurement system |
| **8** | **Metrological function** | Function with administrative and technical responsibility for defining and implementing the measurement system |
| **9** | **Policy** | Intentions and direction of an organization as formally expressed by its top management |
| **10** | **Quality policy** | Policy related to quality |
| **11** | **Vision** | aspiration of what an organization would like to become as expressed by top management |
| **12** | **Mission** | Organization’s purpose for existing as expressed by top management |
| **13** | **Strategy** | Plan to achieve a long-term or overall objective |



**3.6 Terms related to requirement**

| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Object** | Entity, item, or anything perceivable or conceivable |
| **2** | **Quality** | Degree to which a set of inherent characteristics of an object fulfils requirements |
| **3** | **Grade** | Category or rank given to different requirements for an object having the same functional use |
| **4** | **Requirement** | Need or expectation that is stated, generally implied or obligatory |
| **5** | **Statutory requirement** | Obligatory requirement specified by a legislative body |
| **6** | **Regulatory requirement** | Obligatory requirement specified by an authority mandated by a legislative body |
| **7** | **Product-configuration information** | Requirement or other information for product design, realization, verification, operation and support |
| **8** | **Nonconformity** | Non-fulfilment of a requirement |
| **9** | **Defect** | Non-fulfilment of a requirement related to an intended or specified use |
| **10** | **Conformity** | Fulfilment of a requirement |
| **11** | **Capability** | Ability of an object to realize an output that will fulfil the requirements for that output |
| **12** | **Traceability** | Ability to trace the history, application or location of an object |
| **13** | **Dependability** | Ability to perform as and when required |
| **14** | **Innovation** | New or changed object realizing or redistributing value |




**3.7 Terms related to result** 

| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Objective** | Result to be achieved |
| **2** | **Quality objective** | Objective related to quality |
| **3** | **Success** | Achievement of an objective |
| **4** | **Sustained success** | Success over a period of time |
| **5** | **Output** | Result of a process |
| **6** | **Product** | Output of an organization that can be produced without any transaction taking place between the organization and the customer |
| **7** | **Service** | Output of an organization with at least one activity necessarily performed between the organization and the customer |
| **8** | **Performance** | Measurable result |
| **9** | **Risk** | Effect of uncertainty |
| **10** | **Efficiency** | Relationship between the result achieved and the resources used |
| **11** | **Effectiveness** | Extent to which planned activities are realized and planned results are achieved |




**3.8 Terms related to data, information and document** 


| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Data** | Facts about an object |
| **2** | **Information** | Meaningful data |
| **3** | **Objective evidence** | Data supporting the existence or verity of something |
| **4** | **Information system** | Network of communication channels used within an organization |
| **5** | **Document** | Information and the medium on which it is contained |
| **6** | **Documented information** | Information required to be controlled and maintained by an organization and the medium on which it is contained |
| **7** | **Specification** | Document stating requirements |
| **8** | **Quality plan** | Specification of the procedures and associated resources to be applied when and by whom to a specific object |
| **9** | **Record** | Document stating results achieved or providing evidence of activities performed |
| **10** | **Document change plan** | Document specifying the activities to be performed in order to change a document |
| **11** | **Report** | Document stating results of an activity or providing evidence of activities performed |
| **12** | **Project management plan** | Document specifying what is necessary to meet the objectives of a project |
| **13** | **Verification** | Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence, that specified requirements have been fulfilled |
| **14** | **Validation** | Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence, that the requirements for a specific intended use or application have been fulfilled |
| **15** | **Configuration status accounting** | Formalized recording and reporting of product configuration information |



**3.9 Terms related to customer** 


| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Feedback** | Opinions, comments and expressions of interest in a product, a service or a complaint-handling process |
| **2** | **Customer satisfaction** | Customer’s perception of the degree to which the customer’s expectations have been fulfilled |
| **3** | **Complaint** | Expression of dissatisfaction made to an organization relative to its product or service |
| **4** | **Customer service** | Interaction of the organization with the customer throughout the life cycle of a product or a service |
| **5** | **Customer satisfaction code of conduct** | Promises made to customers concerning behavior aimed at customer satisfaction |
| **6** | **Dispute** | Disagreement, arising from a complaint, submitted to a dispute-provider |


**3.10 Terms related to characteristic** 


| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Characteristic** | Distinguishing feature |
| **2** | **Quality characteristic** | Inherent characteristic of an object related to a requirement |
| **3** | **Human factor** | Characteristic of a person having an impact on an object under consideration |
| **4** | **Competence** | Ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results |
| **5** | **Metrological characteristic** | Characteristic which can influence the results of measurement |
| **6** | **Configuration baseline** | Approved product configuration information that establishes the characteristics of a product at a point in time |
| **7** | **Limiting value** | Maximum or minimum specified value for a characteristic |


**3.11 Terms related to determination** 


| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **3.11.2 review** | Determination of suitability, adequacy or effectiveness of an object. |
| **3.11.3 monitoring** | Determining the status of a system, a process or an activity. |
| **3.11.7 inspection** | Determination of conformity to specified requirements. |


**3.12 Terms related to action** 


| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **3.12.1 preventive action** | Action to eliminate the cause of a potential nonconformity. |
| **3.12.2 corrective action** | Action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity and to prevent recurrence. |
| **3.12.3 correction** | Action to eliminate a detected nonconformity. |


**3.13 Terms related to audit** 


| Ref | Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Audit** | Systematic, independent and documented process for obtaining objective evidence and evaluating it objectively to determine the extent to which the audit criteria are fulfilled |
| **2** | **Combined audit** | Audit carried out together at a single auditee on two or more management systems |
| **3** | **Joint audit** | Audit carried out at a single auditee by two or more auditing organizations |
| **4** | **Audit programme** | Set of one or more audits planned for a specific time frame and directed towards a specific purpose |
| **5** | **Audit scope** | Extent and boundaries of an audit |
| **6** | **Audit plan** | Description of the activities and arrangements for an audit |
| **7** | **Audit criteria** | Set of policies, procedures or requirements used as a reference against which audit evidence is compared |
| **8** | **Audit evidence** | Records, statements of fact or other information which are relevant to the audit criteria and verifiable |
| **9** | **Audit findings** | Results of the evaluation of the collected audit evidence against audit criteria |
| **10** | **Audit conclusion** | Outcome of an audit, after consideration of the audit objectives and all audit findings |
| **11** | **Audit client** | Organization or person requesting an audit |
| **12** | **Auditee** | Organization being audited |
| **13** | **Guide** | Person appointed by the auditee to assist the audit team |
| **14** | **Audit team** | One or more persons conducting an audit, supported if needed by technical experts |
| **15** | **Auditor** | Person who conducts an audit |
| **16** | **Technical expert** | Person who provides specific knowledge or expertise to the audit team |
| **17** | **Observer** | Person who accompanies the audit team but does not act as an auditor |
