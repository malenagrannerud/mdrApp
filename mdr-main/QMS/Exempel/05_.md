#### 1. PURPOSE
The purpose of this procedure is to define how Top Management provides leadership, commitment, and oversight of the Quality Management System (QMS) at **[Company Name]**.

#### 2. SCOPE
This SOP applies to all activities related to quality planning, management review, and internal communication within the organization.

#### 3. QUALITY POLICY (ISO 5.3)
- 3.1 The CEO is responsible for establishing and maintaining a Quality Policy that is appropriate for the company’s purpose and regulatory requirements.
- 3.2 The Quality Policy shall be reviewed annually during the Management Review for continuing suitability.
- 3.3 The Quality Policy is maintained as a standalone signed record: `Quality_Policy.pdf`.

#### 4. QUALITY OBJECTIVES (ISO 5.4.1)
- 4.1 The CEO shall ensure that measurable Quality Objectives are established for relevant functions and levels within the organization.
- 4.2 Objectives must support the Quality Policy and be documented in the annual `Quality_Objectives_202X.pdf`.
- 4.3 Progress toward these objectives is measured and reported during Management Reviews.

#### 5. MANAGEMENT REVIEW (ISO 5.6)
- 5.1 **Frequency:** Top Management shall review the QMS at least once every 12 months.
- 5.2 **Participants:** Minimum the CEO, QA Manager, and PRRC.
- 5.3 **Inputs:** The review shall include:
  *   Results of audits (internal/external).
  *   Customer feedback and complaints.
  *   Process performance and product conformity.
  *   Status of CAPAs.
  *   Follow-up actions from previous reviews.
  *   Changes that could affect the QMS (e.g., new regulations).
  *   Post-Market Surveillance data
- 5.4 **Outputs:** The review must result in decisions regarding:
  *   Improvements needed in the QMS.
  *   Changes to the Quality Policy or Objectives.
  *   Resource needs (personnel, tools, budget).
- 5.5 **Records:** Minutes from the meeting shall be maintained as
  `Management_Review_Minutes_202X.pdf`. Records are managed according to SOP-002 Document Control.

#### 6. INTERNAL COMMUNICATION (ISO 5.5.3)
6.1 Management ensures that appropriate communication processes are established.

6.2 Information regarding the effectiveness of the QMS and progress toward Quality Objectives is communicated via [e.g., Monthly All-Hands meetings / Internal Newsletter / Townhall meetings].

## JOB DESCRIPTION & AUTHORITY

| Role | Responsibility (ISO 13485 / MDR) | Authority (Decision Power) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CEO / Top Management** | Establish Quality Policy and Objectives. Provide resources. Lead Management Reviews. | Final approval of budget and QMS strategy. |
| **QA Manager** | Maintain QMS effectiveness. Manage Document Control, Internal Audits, and CAPA. | **Authority to stop production** or release in case of non-conformity. |
| **CTO / Technical Lead** | Design and Development control. Maintain Technical Documentation and Risk Management (ISO 14971). | Approval of technical specifications and design verification/validation. |
| **PRRC (MDR Art. 15)** | Verify device conformity before release. Manage Vigilance and Post-Market Surveillance (PMS). | **Independent authority** to block product release for regulatory reasons. |
| **Operations Manager** | Purchasing, supplier evaluation, and logistics. Ensure traceability and UDI compliance. | Approval of suppliers for the ASL (in coordination with QA). |
| **HR / Admin** | Manage personnel records and coordinate training activities. | Approval of internal training resources and training logs. |
