# Conduct an adverse event analysis in steps
How to conduct the adverse event analysis?

##### Step 1) Initiate the analysis
##### Step 2) Collect the data/facts
##### Step 3) Describe the course of events
##### Step 4) Identify the reasons and analyse the barriers /protection
##### Step 5) Prepare of measures to be taken
##### Step 6) Prepare a final report

## Step 2) Collect the data/facts


- Interview the personnel involved

- Collection of additional relevant information, ex.
  - Patient journal,
  - technical manuals and service records,
  - safety documents,
  - routine descriptions

- Site visit at the actual department

## Step 3) Describe the course of events

The chronological sequence of underlying events

- Every underlying event will be formulated so that a chain of
causes can start out from the event, i.e. the question “WHY”
can be asked.

- Every underlying event will render something that has
happened and that has had significance for the development
of the adverse event.



## Step 4) Identify the reasons and analyse the barriers /protection

### Initial questions
* Was there shortcomings in the written and/or oral communication/
information?
* Was there shortcomings in education and competence?
* Was there shortcomings in the physical or the psychological working
environment?
* Was medical equipment involved in this adverse event?
* Was there shortcomings in barriers/protections?

Ask between each step in the event chain: What barriers/protections there was? Where did these fail?

Ask for each identified barrier/protection: Why did this barrier/protection fail?

Ask in order to identify new barriers/protections: What type of barriers/protections could have prevented the
adverse event?

## Step 5) Prepare of measures to be taken

The analysis team decides if the underlying causes can be
- eliminated
- limited and/or controlled
- accepted

If the underlying causes cannot be eliminated or limited/
controlled, propose measures that limit the consequences
of the negative event

## Step 6) Prepare a final report
The report shall include:
• Description of the course of the events
• The most important underlying causes
• What barriers and protections that have failed or that are
missing
• The proposed measures to be taken
