## DEFINITIONS
- Hazard: Potential source of harm.
- Harm: Physical injury or damage to the health of people, or damage to property or the environment.
- Risk: Combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm.
- Residual Risk: Risk remaining after risk control measures have been implemented.

## 1. PURPOSE
The purpose of this SOP is to establish a systematic process for identifying,
estimating, evaluating, controlling, and monitoring risks associated with medical devices
throughout their entire lifecycle. This ensures that all devices placed on the market are
safe, effective, and achieve a favorable benefit-risk ratio in accordance with ISO 14971:2019 
and MDR 2017/745. 

## 2. SCOPE
This SOP applies to all medical devices manufactured or distributed by [Company Name], covering all stages from initial concept and design through production, post-market clinical use, and final decommissioning/disposal.

## 3. RESPONSIBILITIES
- Process Owner: QA/RA Manager. Responsible for ensuring the risk management framework 
complies with regulatory requirements.
- Project Manager: Responsible for ensuring a Risk Management File (RMF) is established 
for each specific product.
- Risk Management Team: R&D, Clinical, and Quality. Responsible for performing hazard identification and risk estimation.

## 4. THE RISK MANAGEMENT PROCESS

### 4.1 Risk Management Planning
For every device, a Risk Management Plan shall be created. All risk records shall be 
managed in accordance with SOP-001: Document Control and Management. The plan must include:

- The scope of the device and its intended use.
- Assignment of responsibilities.
- Criteria for risk acceptability as in QM-001.
- Plans for verification of risk control measures and post-market surveillance (PMS) activities.

### 4.2 Risk Analysis & Evaluation
The primary tool used is Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA). Any software tools used 
for risk analysis (e.g., Excel templates) must be validated according to SOP-003 before use.
- Hazard Identification: Identify all potential hazards (e.g., biological, electrical, 
software, or use-related).
- Risk Estimation: For each hazard, estimate the Severity (S) and Probability (P) of 
occurrence using a 5x5 matrix.
- Risk Evaluation: Compare the estimated risk against the pre-defined acceptability criteria.

### 4.3 Risk Control
Risk control measures shall be traced to the General Safety and Performance Requirements (GSPR) as defined in MDR Annex I. Risks identified as "Unacceptable" must be mitigated using the following hierarchy:
- Inherent Safety by Design: Eliminate the hazard through engineering.
- Protective Measures: Incorporate alarms or physical barriers.
- Information for Safety: Warnings in the Instructions for Use (IFU) and training.

### 4.4 Benefit-Risk Analysis
After all risk controls are implemented, the team must perform a final 
Benefit-Risk Analysis. The device is only eligible for market release if the clinical 
benefits to the patient outweigh the overall residual risks.

### 4.5 Risk Management Report
A Risk Management Report shall be issued prior to product launch, summarizing 
the results and confirming that the Risk Management Plan has been successfully completed.

### 4.6 Post-Market Surveillance (PMS)
Risk management is an iterative process. Data from production and 
post-market phases (complaints, vigilance, clinical feedback) must be continuously reviewed. 
If new risks are identified, the Risk Management File must be updated immediately.
